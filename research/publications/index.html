Recent stuff

DeepConvSep deep learning repository updated with examples of classical music separation (Bach chorales, four instruments) and with a wrapper which queries RWC instruments sound database for samples.

Various articles on my current work on classical music source separation as a part of the a PHENICX project have been published in Catalan, Spanish, and Romanian. Find out more on MTG's news page.

We submitted a paper on training robust models for classical music source separation, departing from the score. It can be seen as a context-driven data generation/augmentation approach.
M. Miron, J. Janer, and E. Gomez, "Generating data to train convolutional neural networks for low latency classical music source separation" Sound and Music Computing Conference 2017 (submitted)
